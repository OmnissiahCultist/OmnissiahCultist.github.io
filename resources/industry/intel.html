News Sources/Discussions

Infosec News Webcasts on YouTube

  • Hosted by Simply Cyber: Daily Cyber Threat Brief

TL;DR: (Weekdays) Daily hour long webcast starting at 8:00AM EST (streamed on the SC Discord and LinkedIn as well). The stream is roughly an hour focused on news story discussions then open Q&A - news is received from CISO series podcast (linked below).


  • Hosted by CISO Series: Cyber Security Headlines

TL;DR: (Weekdays) Daily briefing on curated list of trending cybersecurity news stories. Videos tend to be under 10 minutes besides the weekly reviews.


  • Hosted by Black Hills Information Security: Talkin' Bout [infosec] News

TL;DR: Live webcast with panelist discussing recent currated cybersecurity news stories. Weekly on Mondays 4:30PM EST.



Trend Reports/Security Researchers

Cybersecurity Trend Reports

  • MITRE's ATT&CK and ENGAGE

TL;DR: ATT&CK stands for Adversay Tactics and Techniques and Common Knowledge is a curated matrix of well known threat-actor strategies. ENGAGE is a seperate matrix (more of a framework) collecting strategies for Blue-Team cyber deception, denial, and adversarial engagement.


  • CrowdStrike's Global Threat Report

TL;DR: An annual report providing in-depth analysis of global cybersecurity threats, adversary groups, and attack trends.


  • OWASP's Top Ten

TL;DR: A regularly updated list of the top security risks to web applications, widely considered an industry standard for web security.


  • IBM's X-Force Threat Intelligence Report

TL;DR: An annual report providing insights into the latest cybersecurity threats and trends, based on real-world data.


Cybersecurity Research Groups

  • Palo Alto Networks's Unit 42

TL;DR: Big name threat intelligence team providing insights on cyber threats, malware, and cybersecurity trends.


  • Google's Multiple Research Teams

TL;DR: Google collectively hosts a variety of research groups focusing on various cybersecurity research. Just to name a few: Project Zero Team, Google Research (Generic Sec/Privacy) Team, Anti-Abuse Team, Jigsaw Team, and others.



Intel Sharing Infrastructure

Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures (CVE) System

  • CVE System's Main Page

TL;DR: The CVE system is maintained by multiple parties and provides unique identifiers for publicly known cybersecurity vulnerabilities, enabling organizations to track and manage them effectively.


  • FIRST's Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS)

TL;DR: The Common Vulnerabilitiy Scoring System (CVSS) is a standardized framework for the CVE rating system. The website also enables orgs to practice their own scoring of threats and is maintained by the Forum of Incident Response and Security Teams (FIRST).


  • NISTS's National Vulnerability Database (NVD)

TL;DR: A U.S. government repository of vulnerability management data, including CVE records and CVSS scores - maintained by the National Institute Of Standards andd Technology (NIST).


  • CISA's Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) Catalog

TL;DR: Another U.S. government repository focusing on tracking actively exploited vulnerabilities based on CVE identifiers - maintained by the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA).


Organizations/Groups Support Networks

  • National Council of ISACs's Main Page

TL;DR: A council of industry-specific Information Sharing and Analysis Centers (ISACs) that facilitate threat intelligence sharing to protect critical infrastructure sectors.
