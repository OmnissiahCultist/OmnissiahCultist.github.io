...Great Platforms!

These are specific cyber security platforms/content creators that are educational and have great communitiy support.
Disclaimer: The following content reflects my personal opinions and subjective experiences. Please keep that in mind before visiting any external links.


Learning Platforms

Mimo - Coding Practice App

  • Website: https://mimo.org

TL;DR: A mobile-first app that is like duolingo for coding.

My thoughts...

I discovered Mimo as an app to help me avoid getting rusty at coding and had a great experience with their UI. Their service is free (with ads) or premium, and this is the link to their website (and no, I am not sponsored). Mimo has several different languages and, in my opinion, does a good job and is very accessible for practicing or even learning a new coding language on the go. The free version's ads are not invasive (as of me writing this in early 2025), and the app has the same feel as Duolingo. I have only experienced using it on iOS, but I believe it is available on most other platforms.

Me Rambling: My thoughts on Duolingo and learning spoken languages are better saved for a separate tangent, but what I can say for Mimo is that the application to actual coding is very direct. In my experience taking learning courses online or in person, the structure of Mimo is very similar to coding courses that slowly build your knowledge of the code while mixing in both guided and unguided coding challenges. I think the accessibility of Mimo as a resource (especially for someone stretched for time) is its biggest selling point. That, combined with its great UI and engaging content, makes it worth mentioning for me.

Malware Traffic Analysis

  • Website: https://www.malware-traffic-analysis.net

TL;DR: Practice site for analyzing malicious pcap traffic with walkthroughs and self-study prompts.

My thoughts...

To call this website an awesome resource is an understatement. This website, by Bradley Duncan, offers a wide variety of pcap files with malicious traffic examples inside. The website is set up for you to pick from a list of packet captures and assume the role of a security analyst as you complete the tasks given (usually reflecting identifying the maliscious traffic and other parts of the capture). The author also provides an answer sheet with some short explanations for each packet (mainly with WireShark), there are some other resources on the site as well. Fantastic resource for anyone wishing to better their skills of packet analysis.


Silicon Dojo

  • YouTube: @elithecomputerguy
  • Website: https://www.silicondojo.com

TL;DR: Pay-what-you-can (intro) IT classes hosted in-person and online — broad technical focus.

My thoughts...

Silicon Dojo is an online educational service hosted by YouTube's Eli The Computer Guy. These courses can be attended live or watched afterward, with a "pay what you can, if you can" policy. The videos cover various broad topics within IT (concepts, networking, cybersecurity, programming, etc.), so there is plenty of content to absorb. In person (if you live nearby), Silicon Dojo hosts physical lectures where resources (like computers) are provided to follow along and engage in labs. Otherwise, there are online class resources too.

I watched a decent amount of Eli's courses, and I can say I have gained a lot from them — especially as someone starting out in IT. Eli's YouTube is now mainly personal vlogs and commentary, but his old videos are still up and contain plenty of relevant teaching content.



Community Platforms

DFIR Diva

  • Website: https://dfirdiva.com

TL;DR: Curated DFIR job boards, free training lists, and professional guidance for forensics careers.

My thoughts...

DFIR Diva provides excellent resources for anyone interested in digital forensics and incident response. The website is a great starting point for building a career in DFIR and getting guidance on how to proceed with learning, training, or certification (with specific recomendations for affordability).


Black Hills Information Security

  • YouTube: @BlackHillsInformationSecurity
  • Website: https://www.blackhillsinfosec.com
  • GitHub: Active Countermeasures
  • Paid/Free Courses: https://www.antisyphontraining.com
  • BHIS Convention: https://wildwesthackinfest.com

TL;DR: Offensive security consultants and educators — webcasts, Antisyphon training, and a great YouTube archive.

My thoughts...

Black Hills InfoSec offers high-quality training and regularly hosts webcasts and podcasts covering various cybersecurity topics, along with other information-rich videos. Much like Simply Cyber, they have a passionate team and a friendly community supporting them. They distribute free tools, host Antisyphon Training, have blogs and resources available, host South Dakota's Wild West Hackin’ Fest cyber convention, and are a full-on cybersecurity provider to top it off. Great community and people focused on moving the industry forward in a positive way offering a variety of learning resources.

Professor Messer

TL;DR: Free and paid certification prep resources — especially known for CompTIA training content.
