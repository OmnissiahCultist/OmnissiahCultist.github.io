Learning Platforms

Mimo - Coding Practice App

  • Website: https://mimo.org

TL;DR: A mobile-first app that makes learning to code feel like a game — great for busy schedules.

My thoughts...

I discovered Mimo as an app to help me not get rusty at coding and had a great experience with their UI. Their service is free(with ads)/premium and this is the link to their website (and no I am not sponsored). Mimo has several different languages and in my opinion does a good job and is very accessible for practicing or even learning a new coding language on the go.


Antisyphon Training

  • Website: https://www.antisyphontraining.com

TL;DR: Pay-what-you-can training that includes full-length videos and hands-on labs — no strings attached.

My thoughts...

Antisyphon’s courses are accessible and relevant. Most are job-skill focused, and their training model makes it easy to dive in. Their live and recorded sessions are always solid.


Hack The Box

  • Website: https://www.hackthebox.com

TL;DR: Offensive-focused platform for hacking virtual machines, red team labs, and skill trees.

My thoughts...

HTB is one of the go-to platforms for anyone interested in penetration testing or ethical hacking. Their system is polished and covers beginner to advanced tiers.


KC7 Cyber Practice

  • Website: https://kc7cyber.com

TL;DR: Interactive scenarios focused on blue team skill-building — geared toward defenders.

My thoughts...

KC7 provides hands-on experience with detection and investigation scenarios. The visual interface is intuitive and makes learning fun.


Malware Traffic Analysis

  • Website: https://www.malware-traffic-analysis.net

TL;DR: Practice site for analyzing malicious pcap traffic with walkthroughs and self-study prompts.

My thoughts...

This site provides real packet captures for malware analysis. The author gives background and walkthroughs — great for refining Wireshark skills.


Silicon Dojo

  • Website: https://www.silicondojo.com
  • YouTube: @elithecomputerguy

TL;DR: Pay-what-you-can IT classes hosted in-person and online — broad technical focus.

My thoughts...

I’ve watched Eli for years. He covers cybersecurity, IT, networking, and coding with a lecture style that’s very digestible. Silicon Dojo also runs physical meetups.


Community Platforms

DFIR Diva

  • Website: https://dfirdiva.com

TL;DR: Curated DFIR job boards, free training lists, and professional guidance for forensics careers.

My thoughts...

DFIR Diva has been instrumental in helping me plan out how to approach digital forensics as a beginner. Their resources are comprehensive and current.


Black Hills Information Security

  • Website: https://www.blackhillsinfosec.com
  • YouTube: @BlackHillsInformationSecurity

TL;DR: Offensive security consultants and educators — webcasts, Antisyphon training, and a great YouTube archive.

My thoughts...

BHIS was one of my first cyber YouTube channel subscriptions. Their content is honest, deep, and usually includes practical tools. Antisyphon is a major plus.


Simply Cyber

  • Website: https://simplycyber.io
  • YouTube: @SimplyCyber

TL;DR: Daily cyber threat briefings, career advice, and free training material curated for newcomers.

My thoughts...

Simply Cyber has been a solid source of day-to-day awareness and encouragement. I like their delivery and consistency, especially for morning briefings.


Professor Messer

  • Website: https://www.professormesser.com
  • YouTube: @ProfessorMesser

TL;DR: Free and paid certification prep resources — especially known for CompTIA training content.

My thoughts...

I used Professor Messer’s materials for Security+ and they helped a lot. His structure is predictable, helpful, and full of clarity.


Individual Content Creators

Tyler Ramsbey

Tyler’s walkthroughs are clean and focused on realism. He brings a calm, practical vibe to each tutorial, and his digital forensics work is stellar.


BlackPerl
